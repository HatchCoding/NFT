Lesson 2: Choose Art Style

What is Procedural Art?

Procedural art (PA) is created randomly by a computer when you have a base image or structure. There is also some randomization that allows it create multiple variations of the art work. Processing.js is often using to create this art form.

Art Example 1: Dragon Egg

This type of PA is consistent and there is a clear base image behind your randomization. In this case, it is a dragon egg all with their own variation.

  1. The base is the dragon egg. There is only one kind of dragon egg. 
  2. Random elements:
    1. Egg colour (16)
    2. Base colour (8)
    3. Egg size (3)
    4. Base colour is 1 or 2 colours (2)
    5. Background colour (4)

Now you have [ 1 x 16 x 8 x 3 x 2 x 4 ]  or 3,072 different versions of the Dragon Egg. Pretty Cool!

Art Example 3: Weird Whales

Weird Whales (actually monetized Digital Art). You saw an image of one of the whales in the previous section. Feel free to explore the entire collection online to see a few of the whales.

The base is the Whale

  1. Random Elements:
    1. Background (8)
    2. Base Colour of Whale (4)
    3. Normal (Blue), Zombie (green), Ape (brown), Alien (cyan)
    4. Eye Accessory (7)
    5. Headgear (17) (e.g. Police Caps, Beanies, Viking Hats)
    6. Mouth Accessory (4)

Now you have 8 * 4 * 7 * 17 * 4 or 15,232 possible variations of a Weird Whale.

AND: Aliens are more 8X rarer than Normal. Police Caps are 6X rarer than Viking Hats

Out of these 15,232 possible combinations, 3,350 of them were created for the collection which is the first instance of a Kawaii pixel whale used in a generative art project with custom on-chain and cryptographically secure provenance.
